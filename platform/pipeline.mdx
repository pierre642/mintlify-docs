The Skult Pipeline view gives you a structured, real-time picture of where every detected opportunity stands in your B2G sales cycle. Because public sector deals move slowly — typically 6 to 18 months from first signal to contract signature — having a clear pipeline with stage-level visibility is essential for sustaining momentum, prioritising actions, and forecasting accurately.

The pipeline is driven by the status and notes you assign to signals in your [Inbox](/platform/inbox). Every status change is reflected here instantly, and the statistics panel shows you exactly how many opportunities sit at each stage.

---

## The B2G sales stages in Skult

Skult maps the natural phases of a public sector sales cycle into six pipeline stages, aligned with the statuses you assign to signals.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Signal Detected">
    A buying signal has appeared in your Inbox — a deliberation, a DOB, a budget vote, or a council minutes passage that indicates a coming procurement. The signal is scored and awaiting your review. Status: **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Qualified">
    You have reviewed the signal, confirmed it matches a real opportunity for your offer, and decided to pursue it. You have read the verbatim quote, assessed the collectivité's size and budget, and identified the relevant contacts. Status: **To Process**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="First Contact">
    You have initiated outreach to at least one decision-maker at the collectivité — by email, phone, or LinkedIn. You are waiting for a response or have scheduled a first call. Status: **In Progress**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Exploratory">
    A conversation is underway. You have confirmed the need, understood the timeline, and identified the key stakeholders. You are mapping the decision-making unit and positioning your offer. Status: **In Progress**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Proposal">
    You have submitted a formal proposal, a letter of intent, or a technical response to an RFI. The collectivité is evaluating your offer or the procurement process has been formally launched. Status: **In Progress**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Closed">
    The opportunity has reached a final outcome — a contract signed, a tender lost, or a decision deferred indefinitely. Document the outcome in a closing note before moving to **Closed** status. Status: **Closed**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The First Contact, Exploratory, and Proposal stages all map to the **In Progress** status in Skult. Use your signal notes to record which sub-stage each opportunity is at. See [Logging notes and updates](#logging-notes-and-updates) below.
</Note>

## Pipeline statistics

The statistics panel at the top of the Pipeline view shows you a real-time count of signals at each status level.

| Status | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **New** | Unreviewed signals awaiting triage. A high New count means your agents are active — reduce it daily. |
| **To Process** | Qualified opportunities waiting for first outreach. This is your immediate action queue. |
| **In Progress** | Active opportunities at some stage of engagement. Monitor this for stagnation — opportunities that do not move in 30 days need attention. |
| **Closed** | Completed opportunities, both won and lost. Review closed signals quarterly to extract learnings. |

<Tip>
  A healthy pipeline has a low **New** count (you triage daily), a growing **In Progress** count (you are actively pursuing opportunities), and a **Closed** count that increases steadily (you are bringing cycles to resolution). If your **To Process** count is high and **In Progress** is low, your outreach cadence needs attention.
</Tip>

## Logging notes and updates

Notes are the backbone of long-cycle B2G pipeline management. Every interaction, every email sent, every call made should be logged as a timestamped note on the signal.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the signal from the Pipeline or Inbox view">
    Click the signal to open its detail panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Notes tab">
    Scroll to the **Notes** section at the bottom of the panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log the interaction">
    Write a concise note describing what happened: the contact reached, the channel used, the key points discussed, the outcome, and the agreed next step with a target date.

    Example: *"10/06 — Called DGS Marie-Claude Fontaine. Confirmed budget approved for H2. She asked for a one-pager by 20/06. Sending tomorrow. Follow up 22/06."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the signal status if the stage has changed">
    If the interaction moved the opportunity to a new stage, update the **Status** dropdown to reflect the current state before saving the note.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Notes are chronologically ordered and timestamped automatically. When you return to a signal after several weeks, the note thread gives you an instant recap of everything that has happened — no CRM archaeology required.
</Info>

## Managing long B2G cycles effectively

B2G sales cycles are uniquely long because public procurement is governed by legal deadlines, council meeting calendars, and budget cycles that do not bend to commercial pressure. Use the following practices to maintain momentum without burning out your contacts.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Align follow-up dates with council calendars">
    French councils typically vote on budgets in November–December (budget primitif) and May–June (budget supplémentaire). Time your follow-up touchpoints to coincide with these moments — your contact has the most internal leverage immediately after a budget vote. Check the council's official agenda on the collectivité's website.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Document every interaction without exception">
    A B2G cycle involving six months and eight contacts across three departments is impossible to manage from memory. Log every email, call, and meeting in the signal's note thread the same day it happens. If you hand the opportunity to a colleague, the notes are their complete briefing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Watch for new signals from the same collectivité">
    A second or third signal from the same organisation is a strong buying intent indicator. Skult will surface it automatically — link it to your existing In Progress signal via a cross-reference note so you do not treat it as a separate cold opportunity.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Re-qualify stagnant opportunities quarterly">
    Any In Progress signal that has not had a note logged in 90 days needs a re-qualification decision. Either renew contact and log a fresh note, or move the signal to Closed with a note explaining why (budget frozen, elections delayed the decision, contact left the role). Do not let stale signals pollute your pipeline.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use Closed signals as competitive intelligence">
    When you lose a tender, log the reason in the closing note: price, incumbent advantage, specification written for a competitor. Over time, this creates a pattern analysis that improves how you qualify future signals from similar collectivités.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## CRM integration for full pipeline visibility

If your team uses a CRM, connecting Skult gives you a single source of truth that spans early-stage signal management and formal deal tracking.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Push signals as deals or opportunities" icon="arrow-right">
    Export a Skult signal directly to your CRM as a new deal (Pipedrive, Salesforce, HubSpot, Attio, Zoho, Odoo, Sellsy). The signal score, verbatim, and collectivité details are mapped to deal fields or activity notes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sync status updates bidirectionally" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Stage changes made in your CRM can be reflected back in Skult, and vice versa, keeping both systems consistent without manual double-entry.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Attach contacts to deals" icon="user-plus">
    Export the decision-maker contacts attached to a signal directly to the linked deal in your CRM, so your team has full contact context in the tool they use every day.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Report on B2G pipeline in your CRM" icon="chart-bar">
    Once Skult data flows into your CRM, your existing pipeline reports and forecasting tools include your B2G opportunities alongside the rest of your commercial activity.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Configure your CRM integration under [CRM Integrations](/integrations/crm). Once connected, the **Export to CRM** button appears on every signal detail view and contact card. See [Contacts](/platform/contacts) for more detail on contact export.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Avoid creating duplicate deals by exporting the same Skult signal to your CRM more than once. Before exporting, search your CRM for the collectivité name to check whether a deal already exists. If it does, add the signal's context as an activity note on the existing deal rather than creating a new one.
</Warning>

## Pipeline best practices summary

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Triage to zero daily" icon="inbox">
    Clear every **New** signal each day. Qualify or dismiss within 24 hours. A signal left in New for a week is a missed window.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Log before you close the tab" icon="pen-to-square">
    Write your interaction note immediately after a call or email. Memory degrades fast — what feels obvious today is opaque in three months.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Plan around the budget calendar" icon="calendar">
    Build your follow-up sequences around the French municipal budget cycle: deliberation season (Sept–Oct), budget primitif vote (Nov–Dec), and budget supplémentaire (May–June).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Review and prune quarterly" icon="scissors">
    Every quarter, review all In Progress signals older than 90 days. Re-qualify or close. A clean pipeline forecasts better than a full one.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
