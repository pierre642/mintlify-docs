Selling to French local governments means navigating a distinct administrative vocabulary. This glossary covers the terms you will encounter most often — from official procurement documents and institutions to the sales concepts that structure a modern B2G approach. Terms are grouped by theme for easier navigation.

<Note>
  Understanding these terms helps you read Skult alerts faster and approach decision-makers with precision. When a signal references a DOB, a PPI, or a MAPA, knowing what each means tells you immediately how far along a project is and who to call.
</Note>

## Procurement Process

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Signal d'achat — Buying signal">
    An early indicator that a collectivité is moving toward a procurement. Buying signals appear in public documents — council minutes, budget debates, deliberations — well before any formal tender is published. They can take the form of a problem described by an elected official, a budget line created for a new project, or a reference to an upcoming investment. Skult's core function is detecting these signals **6 to 18 months before** they reach the BOAMP.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Marché public — Public procurement contract">
    A contract between a public authority and a private supplier for the provision of works, supplies, or services. French public procurement law sets strict rules around competition, transparency, and equal treatment of candidates. The applicable procedure depends primarily on the estimated contract value and the type of purchase.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="AO — Appel d'Offres (Call for tenders)">
    A formal competitive procurement procedure used for contracts above certain thresholds. The contracting authority publishes a tender notice, receives sealed bids, and awards the contract based on defined criteria. By the time an AO is published, the technical direction of the project has usually been set informally. Arriving at the AO stage without prior engagement puts you at a structural disadvantage.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="MAPA — Marché à Procédure Adaptée">
    A simplified procurement procedure used for mid-range contracts that fall below the formal AO threshold (currently €215,000 for services and supplies for local authorities). MAPAs offer the contracting authority more flexibility in how they run the competition. Because they are less visible than formal AOs, they are frequently awarded to suppliers who established contact early — making upstream signal detection especially valuable for this segment.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Gré à gré — Direct award">
    A contract awarded directly to a supplier without a competitive tendering process, permitted for contracts below the MAPA threshold (typically €40,000). Gré à gré contracts never appear on BOAMP. They represent a substantial share of total public procurement volume and are almost exclusively won by suppliers who are already known to the collectivité before the need becomes urgent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sourcing — Pre-market consultation">
    The phase before a formal tender is launched, during which the contracting authority gathers market intelligence from potential suppliers. Participating in sourcing gives you the opportunity to influence the technical specifications before they are locked in the CCTP. Skult signals often indicate when a collectivité is entering a sourcing phase, which is the optimal moment to make contact.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="DOB — Débat d'Orientation Budgétaire">
    An annual debate held in every French local government council before the budget vote, typically in the autumn. The DOB is one of the earliest and most reliable buying signals in the public sector: it is where elected officials discuss investment priorities, infrastructure needs, and multi-year spending plans before any formal commitment exists. A DOB that mentions your offer category is a high-value early alert.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="PPI — Plan Pluriannuel d'Investissement">
    A multi-year investment plan in which a collectivité maps out its capital expenditure over a three-to-five-year horizon. A project appearing in a PPI indicates a genuine long-term commitment with allocated budget — not just a verbal intention. PPI entries are strong buying signals because they reflect decisions already validated at the political level.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Key Documents

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="BOAMP — Bulletin Officiel des Annonces des Marchés Publics">
    The official French journal for public tender notices. All formal public contracts above applicable thresholds must be published in the BOAMP before candidates can submit bids. BOAMP publication marks the beginning of the visible competition phase — by this point, the informal pre-selection has almost always already taken place. Skult detects signals from upstream source documents that precede BOAMP publication by **6 to 18 months**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="DECP — Données Essentielles de la Commande Publique">
    An official open dataset that aggregates key data on all French public contracts above €25,000 — including contract value, winner, award date, and CPV code. DECP data is useful for market analysis, identifying which collectivités have already spent in your category, and benchmarking competitors. It is backward-looking (contracts already awarded) rather than forward-looking, but complements Skult's upstream signal detection for territory research.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="CCTP — Cahier des Clauses Techniques Particulières">
    The technical specification document that forms part of a tender dossier. The CCTP defines precisely what the supplier must deliver — performance levels, technical standards, deliverables, timelines. In B2G sales, your goal is to influence the CCTP before it is finalised, not respond to it after publication. Suppliers who engage during the sourcing phase often see their technical vocabulary and standards reflected in the CCTP.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="RC — Règlement de Consultation">
    The consultation rules document that accompanies a tender. The RC sets out the procedural terms: who can apply, what documents to submit, evaluation criteria, deadlines, and the format of bids. Together with the CCTP, it defines the competitive framework for a given contract. Reviewing the RC helps you assess whether a published tender is genuinely open or has been written around an incumbent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Délibération — Council deliberation">
    A formal decision voted in a municipal or community council session. Deliberations are the official record of decisions made by elected officials and are published in full. They are one of the richest sources of buying signals: a deliberation that authorises a budget envelope for a specific project, approves a multi-year plan, or references a procurement need is an early and verifiable indicator of intent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="PV de conseil — Council meeting minutes">
    The full minutes of a council session, including debate and discussion that precede a formal deliberation. PV de conseils often contain more granular intelligence than the deliberation itself — elected officials describe problems in their own words, reference costs, name failing systems, or discuss competing options. Skult analyses PV to extract these contextual signals before they crystallise into formal procurement actions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Organisations & Roles

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Collectivité territoriale — French local government entity">
    The generic term for a French local government body. France has multiple levels of collectivités: **communes** (municipalities), **départements**, and **régions**. There are approximately 35,000 communes in France, ranging from villages of a few hundred inhabitants to cities of several hundred thousand. All are potential Skult signal sources.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="EPCI — Établissement Public de Coopération Intercommunale">
    An intermunicipal cooperation body created when several communes pool resources to manage shared services — public transport, waste collection, economic development, urban planning. EPCIs include communautés de communes, communautés d'agglomération, communautés urbaines, and métropoles. They often hold larger budgets and more professionalised procurement teams than individual communes, and are key targets for many B2G sellers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="UGAP — Union des Groupements d'Achats Publics">
    France's national public purchasing central body. UGAP negotiates framework contracts with suppliers on behalf of public sector customers, allowing collectivités to purchase directly from the UGAP catalogue without running their own competitive procedure. Being UGAP-referenced dramatically accelerates the purchasing cycle — as illustrated in the New Energy case study, where UGAP referencing combined with early signal detection produced a ×54 ROI.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="DGS — Directeur Général des Services">
    The top administrative officer of a local government, equivalent to a chief operating officer. The DGS is responsible for implementing political decisions and overseeing all administrative departments. In procurement terms, the DGS is frequently the key decision-maker or the person who formally validates supplier relationships — particularly for strategic or cross-departmental purchases. Skult surfaces DGS contacts for each signal.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="DSI — Directeur des Systèmes d'Information">
    The IT director of a local government. The DSI drives technology purchasing decisions — ERP, SIRH, GED, cybersecurity, infrastructure — and is often the primary contact for software and digital service vendors. In larger collectivités, the DSI reports to the DGS and works closely with elected officials responsible for digital transformation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## B2G Sales Concepts

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="B2G — Business-to-Government">
    The practice of selling products or services to public sector entities. B2G differs from B2B in several important ways: procurement is governed by strict legal frameworks, decisions involve both elected officials and administrative officers, purchasing cycles are tied to budget calendars, and a large share of contracts are awarded informally before any public announcement. B2G sales require a different prospecting methodology — one based on reading upstream signals rather than responding to published tenders.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="ICP — Ideal Customer Profile">
    Your definition of the target account that is most likely to buy, most likely to succeed with your product, and most efficient to acquire. In B2G, a well-defined ICP typically specifies: collectivité type (commune, EPCI, département), population or budget size, geographic zone, and any facilitating factors (e.g. UGAP referencing, existing technology stack, recent leadership changes). Configuring your Skult agent starts with translating your ICP into targeting parameters.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
