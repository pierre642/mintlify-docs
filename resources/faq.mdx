Below you'll find answers to the questions our customers ask most often — covering setup, signal quality, data compliance, and how Skult fits into your existing stack.

## Getting Started

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How long does it take to get my first signals?">
    Your first signals arrive within **48 hours** of configuring your agent. During onboarding you define your target sector, geographic zone, and keywords — Skult's AI agents start scanning immediately. Most teams receive actionable alerts the same day they complete setup.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the setup process look like?">
    Setup takes under 48 hours from sign-up to first signal. In your first onboarding session you configure your agent with three parameters:

    1. **Target sector** — your offer category (e.g. HR software, cybersecurity, mobility)
    2. **Target zone** — the regions or departments you cover
    3. **Keywords** — terms that signal a relevant project in council documents

    Once saved, the agent runs continuously and delivers scored alerts straight to your inbox inside Skult.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I run more than one agent on my account?">
    Yes. You can create **multiple agents per account**, each tuned to a different vertical, offer line, or geography. For example, a company selling both energy audits and LED retrofits can run two separate agents with distinct keyword sets and target zones, keeping each sales rep focused on only the signals relevant to their pipeline.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I request a demo before subscribing?">
    Yes. You can request a personalised demo at [skult.ai/demander-une-demo](https://www.skult.ai/demander-une-demo). The session walks you through a live signal feed calibrated to your sector so you can evaluate signal relevance before committing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Platform & Signals

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How is Skult different from BOAMP or France Marchés?">
    BOAMP (Bulletin Officiel des Annonces des Marchés Publics) and France Marchés publish **official tender notices** — documents that appear only once a procurement decision has already been formalised and the contract has been put out to competition. By that point, the technical specification often reflects an informal preference that was settled months earlier.

    Skult works from a completely different data layer. It reads **raw source documents** — municipal council minutes (PV), budget orientation debates (DOB), multi-year investment plans (PPI), and institutional deliberations — and surfaces buying signals **6 to 18 months before** those projects reach BOAMP. You enter the conversation while needs are still being shaped, before competitors even know an opportunity exists.

    <Tip>
      A large share of public contracts never reach BOAMP at all — they are awarded below threshold (gré à gré) or through central purchasing bodies like UGAP. Skult detects those opportunities too.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How are signals scored?">
    Every alert is scored from **0 to 10** based on four factors:

    1. **Signal precision** — is the document vague ("we should look at this") or specific ("envelope of €180k allocated for 2026")?
    2. **Project maturity** — has the need been mentioned once, or is it appearing repeatedly across successive sessions?
    3. **ICP match** — how closely does the collectivité and project type align with your configured Ideal Customer Profile?
    4. **Facilitating factors** — are there elements that lower the sales cycle friction, such as a central purchasing body reference (UGAP) or an existing relationship?

    Scores above 8 typically indicate a high-confidence, time-sensitive opportunity worth contacting immediately.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does Skult cover small rural communes, not just major cities?">
    Yes. Skult monitors **35,000+ French collectivités territoriales**, including small rural communes, intercommunal bodies (EPCI), departments, and regions. Coverage is not limited to large cities or metropolitan areas. Rural communes often represent underserved opportunity — few competitors monitor them, and they frequently award contracts below the formal tender threshold.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What source documents does Skult analyse?">
    Skult's AI agents read public documents published by French local governments on an ongoing basis, including:

    - **Délibérations** — formal decisions voted in municipal and community council sessions
    - **PV de conseil** — full minutes of council meetings
    - **DOB** (Débat d'Orientation Budgétaire) — annual budget orientation debates
    - **PPI** (Plan Pluriannuel d'Investissement) — multi-year investment plans
    - Institutional budgets and financial annexes

    This raw document layer sits upstream of any official procurement publication and is where buying intent first becomes visible.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How is Skult powered technically?">
    Skult is powered by **Mistral AI** for document analysis and signal extraction. The platform also aggregates contact data from **10,000+ official public data sources** to match each signal with the relevant decision-makers at the collectivité.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Contacts & Compliance

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Are the contact emails RGPD compliant?">
    Yes. All contacts on Skult are sourced exclusively from **official public data sources** — government directories, official publications, and institutional websites. Under French law, the professional email addresses of public officials exercising their functions are public information and may be used for legitimate B2B prospecting purposes.

    Skult does not scrape personal inboxes, social media profiles, or private databases. Every contact enrichment is traceable to a verifiable public source.

    <Note>
      Skult is RGPD compliant. If you have specific questions about data processing in the context of your own compliance obligations, your Data Protection Officer can review Skult's data processing agreement (DPA).
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where does Skult source its contact data?">
    Decision-maker contacts are sourced from **10,000+ official public data sources**, including national and local government portals, official gazettes, and public institutional directories. The platform surfaces the right interlocutor for each signal — typically the DGS (Directeur Général des Services), DSI (Directeur des Systèmes d'Information), or the relevant elected official — along with their verified professional email, LinkedIn profile, and phone number where available.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How fresh is the contact data?">
    Skult continuously refreshes contact records to account for elections, internal mobility, and appointments. French local government sees significant turnover — particularly after municipal elections — and stale contact data is a common reason cold outreach fails. The platform flags recent changes in leadership at collectivités so you always reach out to the current decision-maker.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
