## New Energy {#new-energy}

**CEO:** Frédéric Goldzak · **Sector:** Energy solutions · **Reference:** UGAP

### Context

New Energy sells energy solutions to French local governments and holds a UGAP reference — a significant commercial advantage that allows collectivités to purchase directly without a separate tender process. The challenge was not closing deals. It was identifying the right collectivités at the right moment: those already confronting a concrete energy problem and ready to enter a conversation, before the project became visible to the broader market.

### The challenge

Before Skult, New Energy faced the classic B2G prospecting bottleneck: opportunities existed, but remained invisible until someone read the right signals in the right documents.

- **No upstream visibility** — the team could not identify collectivités with real energy problems before projects were formally on the agenda
- **Generic outreach** — without context, every contact started from a cold pitch rather than a recognised pain point
- **Underused UGAP advantage** — the UGAP reference is a powerful shortcut to close, but only if you reach the right collectivité at the right moment
- **Reactive, not proactive** — by the time an opportunity became visible through official channels, the informal decision had often already been taken

### Strategy with Skult

New Energy deployed Skult around a four-step prospecting mechanic:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Detect collectivités with a concrete problem">
    Skult surfaces actionable alerts from deliberations, budget orientation debates (DOB), and institutional publications where elected officials and senior officers describe energy challenges — infrastructure ageing, rising costs, contractual obligations — before any formal project exists.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Qualify the most actionable opportunities">
    Not all alerts carry the same commercial potential. The team focuses on signals where a real budget is being discussed, a decision-maker is identifiable, and the timing aligns with a procurement window. Skult's 0–10 scoring accelerates this triage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact with a contextualised angle">
    Instead of opening with a generic pitch, the sales team references the specific problem already documented in the collectivité's own public records. This shift in posture — from vendor to informed partner — transforms response rates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accelerate the cycle with UGAP referencing">
    Once a need is recognised, New Energy uses its UGAP reference to remove procurement friction. Early signal detection plus a recognised need plus a ready-made purchasing channel is a uniquely powerful combination in B2G.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Nextérité {#nexterite}

**CEO:** Edith Nuss · **Sector:** Smart mobility · **Profile:** Platform for real-time data, supervision, and predictive analytics for transport authorities

### Context

Nextérité helps mobility authorities — metropolitan transport organisations (AOM), métropoles, and regions — manage movement across their territory using real-time data and predictive analytics. In this market, timing is everything. Suppliers who arrive after a budget vote almost always arrive too late: the technical teams have already made an informal choice, sometimes months before the contract is formally launched. For Nextérité, the question was not whether to be competitive on the offer — it was how to arrive at the right moment, with the right context, before decisions were made behind closed doors.

### The challenge

Before Skult, Nextérité's sales team prospected without any visibility into which collectivités were actively thinking about mobility projects.

- **Blind prospecting** — every call started from zero, with no knowledge of whether a project was in progress
- **Manual monitoring was impossible at scale** — tracking council minutes across 500+ target collectivités by hand was not feasible for a lean team
- **Signals arrived too late** — visible signals (published tenders, voted budgets) appeared exactly when informal decisions had already been taken
- **Coverage limited to existing network** — collectivités outside the existing relationship map were never reached, simply because there was no trigger to contact them

> "Quand le conseil communautaire valide un budget, c'est quasiment déjà trop tard. Le service fonctionnel a déjà en tête qu'il va le faire — et souvent, il sait déjà avec qui."
>
> — **Edith Nuss**, CEO, Nextérité

### Strategy with Skult

Nextérité restructured its entire prospecting process around a four-step Skult workflow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Detect pain points before budget votes">
    The Skult agent is configured to surface problems that elected officials describe in council minutes — congestion, excessive operating costs, service failures — before those problems become formal budget deliberations. This is the actionable entry signal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Qualify opportunities on target territories">
    Nextérité refined its ICP around regions and the top 30 metropolitan areas. Skult filters out noise and prioritises high-potential territories, delivering a manageable number of actionable alerts each week rather than hundreds of low-quality notifications.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact with a prepared angle">
    Edith reviews the Skult inbox, classifies opportunities, and passes each commercial rep a complete context brief: identified challenges, project type under consideration, and a suggested approach angle. The call is no longer cold — it opens on the elected official's own stated objectives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cover out-of-network collectivités">
    Skult surfaces projects at collectivités that Nextérité had no prior relationship with. These net-new opportunities — which would never have appeared through the existing network alone — now represent a meaningful share of the pipeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Results

After three months — in a challenging market context marked by an election period and partial project freezes — Nextérité transformed its prospecting process:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="−50%" icon="clock">

  </Card>
  <Card title="+37%" icon="chart-bar">

  </Card>

Beyond the headline numbers:

- **End of unprepared cold calls** — every outreach is now grounded in a real signal and a specific territorial context
- **Prioritised contact schedule** — the team knows exactly where to focus energy each week
- **Refined ICP** — activity data from Skult confirmed the decision to concentrate on regions and the top 30 métropoles

<Tip>
  Nextérité achieved these results during an election period, when many collectivités had partially frozen discretionary spending. Signal-led prospecting is particularly resilient in slow market conditions because it surfaces the collectivités that are actively moving — even when the majority are not.
</Tip>
